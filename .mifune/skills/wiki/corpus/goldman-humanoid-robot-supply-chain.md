# Goldman Humanoid Robot Supply Chain Map

## Summary

An Instagram image re-shares a Goldman Sachs Global Investment Research chart mapping humanoid robot makers and the technology-enabler stack behind them. The durable knowledge is a supply-chain decomposition: humanoid robotics depends on foundation models, GPU/CPU compute, actuators, sensors/vision, dexterous hands, and batteries, creating overlap with AI data centers, grid upgrades, and critical-metals demand.

## Detail

The image headline claims that AI data centers, grid upgrades, and humanoid mass production are converging on scarce metal supply and may create a multi-year critical-metals squeeze. Treat that market claim as provisional: the image is a social repost with a truncated Substack link, while the embedded chart cites Goldman Sachs Global Investment Research as the chart source.

The chart’s useful structure is the bill-of-materials view of humanoid robots. On the demand side it lists robot makers including Tesla, Figure AI, Agility Robotics, Apptronik, Boston Dynamics, Sanctuary AI, Unitree, UBTECH, Xiaomi, XPENG, BYD, CATL, GAC, HUAWEI, and many private or China-based makers. On the supply side it groups enablers by layer: foundation models (Tesla, NVIDIA, OpenAI, Alphabet, Meta, iFlytek, Huawei); GPU/CPU (NVIDIA, Intel); actuator assembly (Sanhua, Tuopu); rotary and linear actuators; dexterous hands; sensors; vision/LiDAR; and batteries (CATL, Samsung SDI, Panasonic).

For analysis, reuse the map as a due-diligence checklist rather than as a stock recommendation. A robotics supply-chain scan should ask: which subsystem is bottlenecked; which suppliers are public vs. private; which parts are shared with EVs, data centers, or grid equipment; and which metals or components become constrained if humanoid volumes rise. The chart also implies that humanoid robotics is not a single vertical: it is a convergence of AI compute, precision motion, sensing, batteries, and advanced manufacturing.

Limitations: the image resolution makes some small company names uncertain, the Instagram caption is promotional, and the chart itself notes that its company list is not exhaustive. Preserve the raw snapshot for OCR verification before making financial or sourcing decisions.
